## The Story

The Supreme Court has overturned **Humphrey's Executor v. United States (1935)** — a 91-year-old precedent that gave commissioners of independent federal agencies (FTC, NLRB, SEC, FCC, EEOC, etc.) protection from being fired by the president except for cause.

**Trump v. Slaughter** (6-3, Roberts majority) held that the President has constitutional authority to remove FTC commissioners at will. The ruling explicitly overruled Humphrey's Executor, declaring that the 1935 framework "has not withstood the test of time."

**What actually happened:** In March 2025, Trump fired Democratic FTC Commissioners Rebecca Kelly Slaughter and Alvaro Bedoya. They sued, won reinstatement in lower court. The administration appealed. SCOTUS sided with Trump, wiping out 90 years of independent agency precedent.
